What Stood Out

The main signals visible at that point

Browser and computer use enter product language

Anthropic's computer-use work and OpenAI's Operator pushed real-environment interaction beyond the lab-demo category. Still limited, but portal operations and screen-based tasks are clearly entering the official product conversation.

Orchestration is moving from notebooks to runtimes

AutoGen v0.4 reframed multi-agent conversation as something closer to a durable runtime than a prompt-chain experiment. The important question becomes who plans, who executes, and where the workflow is allowed to stop.

Evaluation and monitoring start to stand up as a separate layer

Google's Vertex AI messaging makes evaluation look less like a post-build add-on and more like part of the design surface. Teams are being pushed to separate experimentation from measurement.

Use Cases

Use cases that already looked practical at that time

Portal-crossing operations assistants

  • Traverse several admin portals, gather the needed state, and prepare update candidates or drafts.
  • Keeping the final confirm step with a human makes computer-use automation much easier to govern.

Codebase investigation assistants

  • Start from an issue or incident and trace relevant files, tests, and prior diffs across the repo.
  • These flows benefit from durable execution so intermediate findings and tool calls can be inspected and replayed.

Operating Implications

What had to be decided first in design, evaluation, and operations

Observed implication

By February, the separation line was starting to move from capability alone toward how much of the execution path could be observed and replayed.

  • Separate read permissions from write permissions instead of treating them as one class of capability.
  • Maintain replayable tasks that exercise browser or terminal interaction end to end.
  • Keep tool-call histories and document the fallback steps when a run fails.

Key Takeaway

The provisional conclusion that could already be drawn

Agents that touch browsers or terminals cannot be judged on model quality alone. Durable orchestration and observability are becoming the real entry requirements.
