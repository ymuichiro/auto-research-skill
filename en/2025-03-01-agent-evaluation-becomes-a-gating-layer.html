What Stood Out

The main signals visible at that point

More benchmarks expose how coarse most evaluation remains

AgentBench, GAIA, SWE-bench, OSWorld, and BrowserGym all show that single-question testing is too coarse. That also exposes the risk of running an internal proof of concept without a task set of similar granularity.

Official guidance is assuming tracing and guardrails

Anthropic's effective-agents guidance and Google's evaluation messaging both reinforce the need to separate prompt tuning from execution visibility and failure handling. The focus is shifting from answer quality to operational quality.

Scope selection matters more than another model swap

The available evidence suggests that teams who scope the task narrowly get closer to practical results even when using similar models. In deployment decisions, what the agent is not allowed to do is becoming as important as what it can do.

Operating Implications

What had to be decided first in design, evaluation, and operations

Observed implication

The key requirement here is not another model bake-off but a clear definition of failure on the team's own representative tasks.

  • Measure completion and reproducibility across roughly ten representative business tasks.
  • Retain traces that let a human inspect where and why a failed run broke down.
  • Define escalation rules in advance so ambiguous cases are not over-automated.

Key Takeaway

The provisional conclusion that could already be drawn

By March 2025, the real deployment gap was already opening less on flashy capability and more on whether failure could be measured and contained.
