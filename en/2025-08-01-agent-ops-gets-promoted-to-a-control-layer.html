What Stood Out

The main signals visible at that point

Operational visibility is moving toward purchase criteria

Across Foundry GA, Developer Essentials, OpenAI's Responses API updates, and Google's MCP integrations, the common need is visibility into what happened. Traces and run history are becoming part of product selection.

Tool governance matters independently from model quality

Which tools are allowed, which actions require review, and how failures are rolled back remain hard questions even when the model is strong. AgentOps is becoming important as the layer that governs those controls.

Teams that can operate agents cleanly are starting to outperform

Even on similar stacks, teams that can inspect traces and improve workflows iterate faster. Deployment differences increasingly come from operating discipline rather than model choice.

Use Cases

Use cases that already looked practical at that time

Support case operations

  • Automate classification, known-case retrieval, and response drafting for incoming cases.
  • When traces are available, misclassification or bad retrieval can be inspected after the fact.

IT operations diagnostic support

  • Combine alerts, runbooks, and recent changes to summarize likely causes for an incident.
  • Run histories make repeat incidents easier to compare and reproduce.

Operating Implications

What had to be decided first in design, evaluation, and operations

Observed implication

By August, the key team capability was no longer prompt cleverness but the ability to inspect traces and iterate on the workflow.

  • Retain traces, session state, and tool-call history as default operational data.
  • Treat review screens and approval UIs as part of the operating platform, not as external afterthoughts.
  • Define retry rules and recovery paths for failed runs.

Key Takeaway

The provisional conclusion that could already be drawn

By August 2025, differences between agent platforms were increasingly judged not by what they could do in ideal conditions, but by what became visible when things went wrong.
